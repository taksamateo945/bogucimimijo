Charissa Thompson is an American television host and sportscaster serving for Fox Sports. She previously worked for ESPN, Versus, GSN, and the Big Ten Network.

Charissa Thompson’s Bio & Family: Age, Parents, Education, Height

The 38-years-of-age media personality was born as raised as Charissa Jean Thompson on 4 May 1982 in Seattle, Washington, the United States. She is the youngest of three children of her parents, Scott Thompson and Cathy Thompson.

Charissa Thompson wishes Mother’s Day to her mom on 11 May 2020 (Charissa’s Instagram)

About 5 feet and 6 inches (1.68 meters) tall in height, Charissa wanted to become a broadcaster from an early age. For her early education, she attended Inglemoor High school in Kenmore, Washington. Later on, she moved to California and joined community college. Eventually, Charissa transferred to the University of California, Santa Barbara where she graduated with a B.A. in Law and Society in 2004.

Media Career: ESPN, Fox Sports

In the early part of her career, Charissa appeared on various college sports shows on Big Ten Network and Fox Sports Net (FSN) when she joined the network in 2007. Soon, she became a National Football League (NFL) sideline reporter for NFL on Fox.

Back in 2010, She covered the FIFA World Cup in South Africa for Yahoo! Sports. It was later in June 2011 when Charissa joined ESPN. Immediately, she co-hosted the show, Numbers Never Lie, with Michael Smith which debuted on 12 September 2011. Charissa was the co-host of SportsNation along with Marcellus Wiley until her departure from ESPN for Fox Sports in June 2013.

Upon her return to Fox Sports, she became the host of Fox Sports Live on the new Fox Sports 1 network on 17 August 2013. Moreover, she also presented for Fox NFL Kickoff.
