Who Is Carrie Underwood?

Carrie Underwood is one of the most successful and influential country music artists of the 21st century.

She rose to fame after winning the fourth season of the TV show American Idol in 2005, and since then she has released eight studio albums, four compilation albums, and more than 30 singles.

Carrie Underwood Career

Carrie Underwood’s music career began with the release of her first single, “Inside Your Heaven”, on June 14, 2005.

The song debuted at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, making her the only country artist to achieve this feat and the only solo country artist in the 2000s to have a number-one song on the Hot 100.

The song also topped the Canadian Singles Chart and was certified platinum by the RIAA.

The album was a huge commercial and critical success, selling over nine million copies worldwide and becoming the best-selling solo female debut album in country music history.

The album also spawned four number-one singles on the Billboard Hot Country Songs chart: “Jesus, Take the Wheel”, “Don’t Forget to Remember Me”, “Wasted”, and “Before He Cheats”.

The latter two songs also crossed over to the pop charts and became top-ten hits on the Billboard Hot 100 chart.

The album earned Carrie three Grammy Awards in 2007: Best New Artist, Best Female Country Vocal Performance for “Jesus, Take the Wheel”, and Best Country Song for “Jesus, Take the Wheel”.

Carrie followed up her debut album with Carnival Ride, which was released on October 23, 2007.

The album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart with over half a million copies sold in its first week.

It also produced four number-one singles on the Hot Country Songs chart: “So Small”, “All-American Girl”, “Last Name”, and “Just a Dream”.

The album also featured a duet with Randy Travis called “I Told You So”, which reached number two on the country chart and won a Grammy Award for Best Country Collaboration with Vocals in 2010.
